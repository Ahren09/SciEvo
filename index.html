Abstract

Understanding the creation, evolution, and dissemination of scientific knowledge is crucial for bridging diverse subject areas and addressing complex global challenges such as pandemics, climate change, and ethical AI. Scientometrics, the quantitative and qualitative study of scientific literature, provides valuable insights into these processes. We introduce SciEvo, a longitudinal, continuously updating scientometric dataset with over two million academic publications, along with comprehensive content information and citation graphs, to enable cross-disciplinary analyses of scientific evolutions.

Using the dataset, we conduct a longitudinal study spanning over 30 years to explore key questions in scientometrics: the evolution of academic terminology, citation patterns, and interdisciplinary knowledge exchange. Our findings reveal critical insights, such as disparities in epistemic cultures, knowledge production modes, and citation practices. For example, rapidly developing, application-driven fields like Large Language Models exhibit significantly shorter citation age (2.48 years) compared to traditional theoretical disciplines like oral history (9.71 years).

Getting Started

1. Access the Dataset

Load the SciEvo dataset directly from HuggingFace with a simple Python command:

from datasets import load_dataset
dataset = load_dataset("Ahren09SciEvo")

2. Use Analysis Tools

Leverage our comprehensive analysis toolkit for scientometric research:

python analysis/analyze_citation.py --feature_name title
python analysis/keyword_extraction_llm.py --year 2020

3. Generate Insights

Create visualizations and conduct longitudinal analyses of scientific evolution:

python visualization/plot_keyword_traj.py

Timeline of Scientific Evolution

1990s: Theoretical Foundations

Focus on foundational areas like Quantum Field Theory, Particle Physics, and Computational Complexity. Research emphasized theoretical understanding and mathematical rigor.

2000s: Computational Methods

Shift towards data-driven methodologies with the rise of optimization, graph theory, and early machine learning approaches.

2010s: Deep Learning Revolution

Emergence of Deep Learning, Neural Networks, and Reinforcement Learning, constituting over 50% of top keywords by decade's end.

2020s: AI and Societal Impact

Focus on Large Language Models, Transformers, and societal challenges like COVID-19, Fairness, and Ethical AI.
